Quaid e Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

1876 to 1948

Founder Of Pakistan

The founder of Pakistan, Muhammad Ali Jinnah(1876–1948), played a pivotal role in establishing the nation as an independent state for Muslims in 1947. A lawyer and politician by profession, Jinnah led the Indian National Congress initially but later became the leader of the All-India Muslim League. His vision for Pakistan was deeply rooted in the belief that Muslims in the Indian subcontinent needed a separate nation to preserve their identity and rights. Known as "Quaid-e-Azam" or "Great Leader," Jinnah’s relentless advocacy led to the creation of Pakistan, marking the end of British colonial rule in India. His leadership, diplomacy, and determination are celebrated annually on Pakistan's Independence Day, August 14.
